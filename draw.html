Character Design

Character design is a passion I have had for many years. Whenever I am creating a new story, I am often more drawn to the different people and relationships I can create than some of the other elements. This passion has led me to create many designs over the years, many I use in my free time for a variety of games I play with friends.

While my designs tend to be more complicated then ones that would be made for media like cartoons or video games, I still like to use basic elements like shapes and colors to create starting points for my designs. Which I then add onto, taking inspiration from a variety of fashion and genres.

Aside from the characters made for my table top roleplaying game, I’ve also been slowly developing a fantasy story centered around a musician and a sailor searching for their deceased captain. The plot would explore their experience with grief and tackle the way society tends to strip the deceased of their complex personality and identity.
This concept is incredably character focused, which is why I put a lot of thought into their designs.

Additional Character Artwork

Sarah

Occasionally, I am inspired by pieces of media to create art. This piece specifically depicts a very memorable scene from Jim Henson’s 1986 movie, Labyrinth. I love using layers within my drawings to create overlapping colors and textures that really bring life into the art. This drawing was a challenge in creating those different textures that make up the different parts of the dress while maintaining the consistent coloring.

Southern Nights

Since a lot of my drawings tend to focus on the figures within the art, I occasionally add graphic design elements like typography to create more of a poster-like art piece. This work depicts two of my own original characters with the title and lyric from the song Southern Nights by Glen Campbell.
